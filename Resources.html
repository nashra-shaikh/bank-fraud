Books

Essentials of Online payment Security and Fraud Prevention

Author: By David A. Montague

"Essentials of Online Payment Security and Fraud Prevention" is a concise guide focusing on the critical aspects of safeguarding online transactions. It covers the basics of online payment systems, various types of payment fraud, and essential security technologies like encryption and SSL. The book also provides practical fraud detection and prevention strategies, insights into regulatory compliance, and real-world case studies. Ideal for professionals and consumers, it offers actionable tips to enhance security and protect against online fraud.

Practical Fraud Prevention

Author: By Gilit Saporta, Shoshana Maraney

"Practical Fraud Prevention" is a hands-on guide designed to help businesses and individuals protect against fraud in the digital age. The book covers various types of fraud, including identity theft, payment fraud, and account takeover, and provides actionable strategies to detect and prevent these threats. It emphasizes the use of data analytics, machine learning, and other modern tools to combat fraud effectively. With real-world examples and practical tips, this book is an essential resource for anyone looking to safeguard their operations against fraud.

Can You Spot a Phishing Scam?

This blog highlights the growing threat of online scams, particularly phishing, as more people use online banking. It emphasizes that scammers often impersonate banks via fraudulent emails, texts, phone calls, and payment app messages to steal personal information. Banner Bank, alongside the American Bankers Association, aims to educate clients on spotting these scams with the key message: "Banks Never Ask That." By recognizing suspicious requests, such as those asking for personal information or account verification, clients can better protect themselves from fraud.

Tips to Prevent Online Fraud

Businesses face increasing risks from online fraud, including data breaches and phishing attacks. To protect sensitive information, it's crucial for businesses—especially small ones without dedicated IT teams—to adopt key security practices. These include keeping financial data separate by using dedicated workstations, verifying the legitimacy of requests for personal information, maintaining strong password security, avoiding phishing attempts, and protecting computers with updated antivirus software, anti-spam, and firewalls. Regularly updating operating systems and browsers also enhances protection against cyber threats.
